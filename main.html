### *Bill Proposal: Strategic Enhancement and Modernization of India's Defence Forces (2024-2029)*

        *Preamble:*
        This bill outlines the strategic use of the ₹6.21 lakh crore defense budget for 2024-25, focusing on modernizing India’s armed forces, enhancing domestic defense production, and integrating global expertise in training and technology. The goal is to strengthen India’s defense capabilities, ensuring preparedness against contemporary and future threats.
        
        ---
        
        #### *Section 1: Capital Outlay and Modernization*
        
        *1.1. Capital Allocation*
        - *Budget Allocation:* ₹172,000 crore (27.66% of the total defense budget) will be dedicated to capital expenditure, including procurement of advanced technology and equipment.
          
        *1.2. Procurement Strategy*
        - *Domestic Focus:* 75% of the modernization budget will be earmarked for domestic procurement, promoting self-reliance under the Aatmanirbhar Bharat initiative. 
        - *International Collaboration:* Engage in strategic partnerships with countries such as the United States, Israel, and France to procure cutting-edge military technologies (e.g., drones, missile systems, cyber defense tools).
        
        *1.3. Integrated Capability Development Plan (ICDP)*
        - *Objective:* Transition from individual service-specific modernization plans to a holistic, joint-service approach.
        - *Execution:* Develop a 10-year plan aligning military capability development with budgetary commitments, ensuring balanced growth across all services【7†source】【8†source】.
        
        ---
        
        #### *Section 2: Training and Personnel Development*
        
        *2.1. Global Training Programs*
        - *Exchange Programs:* Establish bilateral training agreements with advanced militaries (e.g., U.S., U.K., and Israel) focusing on cyber warfare, AI in defense, and asymmetric warfare.
        - *Special Forces:* Expand the training of India’s Special Forces in collaboration with NATO countries, focusing on counter-terrorism, urban warfare, and high-altitude operations.
        
        *2.2. Leadership and Tactical Training*
        - *War Games and Exercises:* Conduct regular joint military exercises with global partners to simulate real-world scenarios, enhancing tactical acumen and interoperability.
        - *Defense Academies:* Upgrade training modules in Indian defense academies by incorporating best practices and methodologies from top military institutions globally (e.g., West Point, Sandhurst).
        
        *2.3. Agnipath Scheme Expansion*
        - *Budget Allocation:* ₹5,207.28 crore for the Army, with corresponding increases for the Navy and Air Force.
        - *Program Enhancement:* Introduce specialized training tracks under Agnipath for recruits, including cyber defense, drone operation, and battlefield medicine【7†source】.
        
        ---
        
        #### *Section 3: Research and Development (R&D)*
        
        *3.1. DRDO Funding*
        - *Budget Allocation:* Increase DRDO's budget to at least 5% of the total defense budget, amounting to approximately ₹31,097 crore.
        - *Collaborative Projects:* Foster joint R&D projects with international defense firms and research institutions to develop indigenous high-tech weaponry and defense systems.
        
        *3.2. Innovation Hubs*
        - *Defense Innovation Unit:* Establish a Defense Innovation Unit that partners with global tech giants and startups to incubate and accelerate the development of next-gen defense technologies.
        
        *3.3. Cyber Defense Initiative*
        - *Cyber Command:* Allocate funds to establish a dedicated Cyber Command within the Indian Armed Forces, staffed with personnel trained in collaboration with global cybersecurity leaders.
        - *R&D Focus:* Prioritize the development of AI-driven cyber defense mechanisms and quantum-resistant encryption technologies【8†source】.
        
        ---
        
        #### *Section 4: Border and Infrastructure Development*
        
        *4.1. Border Roads Organisation (BRO)*
        - *Budget Allocation:* ₹6,500 crore allocated to BRO for enhancing border infrastructure, reflecting a 30% increase from the previous year.
        - *Projects:* Prioritize the construction of all-weather roads and strategic rail links in border areas with China and Pakistan.
        
        *4.2. Forward Operating Bases (FOBs)*
        - *Infrastructure Upgrade:* Invest in upgrading existing FOBs and establishing new ones along the Line of Actual Control (LAC) to ensure rapid deployment of forces.
        
        *4.3. Smart Border Management*
        - *Technology Integration:* Implement advanced surveillance systems, including drones, sensors, and satellite-based monitoring, along critical border areas.
        
        ---
        
        #### *Section 5: Financial Oversight and Accountability*
        
        *5.1. Budget Review Committee*
        - *Establishment:* Form a Defense Budget Review Committee comprising representatives from the Ministry of Finance, Ministry of Defence, and independent defense experts.
        - *Function:* Conduct quarterly reviews of budget utilization, ensuring alignment with strategic goals and efficient use of resources.
        
        *5.2. Transparency and Reporting*
        - *Public Disclosure:* Publish an annual report detailing the progress of modernization projects, procurement, and training initiatives, accessible to the public and stakeholders.
        
        ---
        
        *Conclusion:*
        This bill aims to strategically utilize the defense budget for 2024-25 to enhance India’s military capabilities through modernization, training, and international collaboration. It emphasizes self-reliance while leveraging global expertise to address current and future security challenges.
